# Reference: Enrolment Query

## When to use this reference
Someone has decided (or close to decided) to enrol and is asking final practical questions before pulling the trigger. Their language is decided, not exploratory. Typical signals:
- "Ready to enrol — couple of quick questions"
- "I want to sign up for the next cohort — what's the next step?"
- Questions about intake dates, payment plans, deposit, what's included, materials access
- They've already had the discovery conversation, watched the webinar, or read the page

NOT for:
- People still deciding whether the course is right for them (use `student-member-question.md`)
- Anyone asking about refund / cancellation terms in a way that suggests doubt (treat carefully — could be a complaint dressed up as a question; lean toward `complaint-or-concern.md` if in doubt)

## Voice and tone rules
- Crisp, friendly, professional. They're ready to act — don't slow them down with fluff.
- British English. Contractions throughout.
- Lead with the practical answers in a clean format. A short bulleted list of their questions, answered, is fine here (this is the one scenario where bullets are welcome).
- Make the next step obvious and easy — a payment link, an enrolment form, or a calendar link to book the spot.
- Confirm the spot is theirs once they act — don't leave them wondering whether it went through.

## What to do
- Answer each of their questions in order, short and clear.
- For anything we don't yet know (intake date, deposit, payment plan terms), use a clearly marked placeholder like `[INTAKE DATE]`, `[DEPOSIT AMOUNT]`, `[PAYMENT PLAN TERMS]` and flag them.
- Make the next step a single click — name the link, say what they'll see when they click it.
- Add one short reassuring line: what happens after they enrol (welcome email, what they'll receive and when).

## What NOT to do
- Don't re-pitch the course. They're already sold — don't talk them back into deciding.
- Don't ask them to "jump on a call first" unless there's a real reason. If they can self-serve, let them.
- Don't bury the link to enrol at the bottom of a wall of text.
- Don't invent specifics. If the next intake date isn't fixed, say "I'll confirm the exact date with you by [DATE]" rather than guessing.

## Reference reply

> Subject: Re: [their subject]
>
> Hi [FirstName],
>
> Good to hear you're ready — quick answers below.
>
> - **Next intake:** [INTAKE DATE]. Enrolment closes [CLOSE DATE].
> - **Payment:** [PRICE] in full, or a [PAYMENT PLAN TERMS, e.g. "3-instalment plan"]. Both options are on the enrolment page.
> - **What's included:** [WHAT'S INCLUDED — e.g. weekly live sessions, recordings, project work, 1:1 coaching, ADN network access].
> - **Access to previous material:** [YES/NO + WHAT — e.g. "yes, you'll get the full library of past guest lectures the day you enrol"].
>
> The enrolment link is here: [ENROLMENT LINK]. Once you've signed up you'll get a welcome email within the hour with the joining details and the first bit of pre-work.
>
> Any final questions, send them over and I'll come straight back. Otherwise, see you on the inside.
>
> Best wishes,
> Yasmin

## Drafting checklist (must all pass before showing the draft)
- [ ] Opener is `Hi [FirstName],` — not "Hello", not "Hey".
- [ ] Each of their actual questions is answered (in the order they asked, where possible).
- [ ] Bulleted list is used for the practical answers (this is the one scenario where bullets are welcome).
- [ ] Enrolment link / next-step link is named and obvious — not buried.
- [ ] No re-pitching the course.
- [ ] No invented dates, prices, or terms — placeholders flagged at the end.
- [ ] One short line confirming what happens after they enrol (welcome email etc.).
- [ ] British English. Contractions throughout. No exclamation marks.
- [ ] Sign-off is `Best wishes,` then `Yasmin` on a new line.
- [ ] Length: tight. Answers + link + sign-off, that's it.

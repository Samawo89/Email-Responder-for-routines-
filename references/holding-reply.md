# Reference: Holding Reply

## When to use this reference
Yasmin needs to acknowledge an email fast without giving a full answer. Two cases:
1. **Email needs processing** — they've sent availability options, a decision to make, materials to read, or a multi-part question. A warm "got it, more soon" is better than a delay.
2. **Yasmin explicitly asks** for a "holding reply" or to "buy me time".

NOT for:
- Complaints (use `complaint-or-concern.md`)
- Refund requests (use `refund-request.md`)
- Anything where the person needs an answer now to act (don't stall someone who needs the intake date today)

## Voice and tone rules
- Warm, brief, human. Two short paragraphs maximum.
- British English. Contractions throughout.
- Acknowledge what they sent specifically — not "thanks for your email".
- Give them a real timeframe, not "soon" or "asap". "By end of day Thursday" or "in the next 48 hours" — and only commit to one Yasmin can actually hit.
- Don't apologise for the delay if there isn't one yet. This is a normal, professional acknowledgement.

## What to do
- Confirm we've got their email and have read it.
- Name one specific thing they sent so they know we read it (a date they proposed, a question they asked, a document they attached).
- Give a clear timeframe for the proper reply — and stick to one that's realistic.
- Sign off cleanly.

## What NOT to do
- Don't try to answer any of their actual questions in the holding reply. That's the point of the holding — full answer comes later.
- Don't use "soon", "ASAP", "shortly" — give a real day or window.
- Don't open with "Apologies for the delay" if there hasn't been one.
- Don't promise a call slot you haven't actually checked.

## Reference reply

> Subject: Re: [their subject]
>
> Hi [FirstName],
>
> Got your email — and the [specific thing: "three slots you sent", "deck attached", "the question about [X]"]. Thanks for the detail.
>
> I want to give this a proper response rather than rush one, so I'll come back to you with everything by [SPECIFIC TIMEFRAME, e.g. "end of day Thursday"]. If anything moves on your end before then, just send it across.
>
> Best wishes,
> Yasmin

## Drafting checklist (must all pass before showing the draft)
- [ ] Opener is `Hi [FirstName],` — not "Hello", not "Hey".
- [ ] One specific thing from their email is named back.
- [ ] No actual answer is given to their question(s) — this is a holding reply only.
- [ ] A specific timeframe is given (a day or window), not "soon" / "ASAP" / "shortly".
- [ ] The timeframe is realistic for Yasmin to hit — flag if unsure.
- [ ] No unnecessary apology for delay.
- [ ] British English. Contractions throughout. No exclamation marks.
- [ ] Sign-off is `Best wishes,` then `Yasmin` on a new line.
- [ ] Length: two short paragraphs maximum.

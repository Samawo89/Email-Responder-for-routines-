# Reference: Partnership / Collab Inquiry

## When to use this reference
Someone external is reaching out about working together. Three flavours:
- **Register A — Cold pitch**: A founder, agency, tool vendor, or coach we don't know, pitching a partnership / collab / sponsorship.
- **Register B — Media**: A podcast host, journalist, or content creator inviting Yasmin on a show or quoting her in a piece.
- **Register C — Warm club inbound**: A football club, academy, or scout body reaching out — usually because someone there saw FCC or knows a student. This is the highest-value bucket.

The default posture across all three is **YES, let's have a conversation**. We don't decline at the email stage — we get them on a call and figure it out from there. The only exception: if the pitch is obviously off-brand or extractive (lead-gen pitches, "exposure" offers, MLM-style asks). In those cases, flag to Yasmin — we don't have a polite-decline reference yet.

## Voice and tone rules
- Warm and curious. Genuinely interested but not eager.
- British English. Contractions throughout.
- Match their energy on formality — Register A and B can be a touch more crisp; Register C (clubs) should be the warmest of the three.
- Show you've read their message — reference one specific thing back to them.
- Make the call easy. Always propose a route to a 20–30 minute conversation.
- Never name-drop other clubs or partners by name without checking with Yasmin first.

## What to do
- Acknowledge what they actually proposed (not "thanks for reaching out").
- Say yes to a conversation in plain terms.
- Propose a route: a Calendly link, or 2–3 slots, or "send me a couple of times that work for you".
- Keep it short — three paragraphs is plenty.

## What NOT to do
- Don't pitch FCC back at them at length. The call is for that.
- Don't ask them to fill out a form or send a deck before talking. Lower the friction, not raise it.
- Don't commit to a specific outcome (sponsorship, partnership, cohort placement) — just commit to the conversation.
- Don't promise to "loop in the team" if there is no team. Be honest about who they'll be talking to (Yasmin).

## Reference replies

### Register A — Cold pitch (founder, agency, vendor, coach)

> Subject: Re: [their subject]
>
> Hi [FirstName],
>
> Thanks for the note — and for being specific about [name the thing they actually proposed]. The shape of it makes sense as something worth a proper conversation.
>
> Quickest way to do that is probably a 20-minute call. Grab a slot here: [CALENDAR LINK] — or if nothing fits, send a couple of times that work and I'll fit around it.
>
> Before then, if there's a one-pager or anything you've already written up, feel free to send it across. Not required.
>
> Best wishes,
> Yasmin

### Register B — Media (podcast, journalist, content creator)

> Subject: Re: [their subject]
>
> Hi [FirstName],
>
> Thanks for thinking of me — and yes, [their show / their piece] sounds like a good fit, particularly the angle on [reference the specific angle they mentioned].
>
> Happy to do it. Quickest way is probably a 20-minute pre-chat so we can shape what's actually useful for your audience. Grab a slot here: [CALENDAR LINK].
>
> If you've got a brief or running order already, send it across whenever — I'll have read it before we speak.
>
> Best wishes,
> Yasmin

### Register C — Warm club inbound (academy, scout body, FA-adjacent)

> Subject: Re: [their subject]
>
> Hi [FirstName],
>
> Really good to hear from you, and thanks for reaching out directly. The way you've described [the thing they want to discuss — e.g. "building analyst capability across the academy age groups"] is something we've thought hard about, so this is well-timed at our end.
>
> I'd love to find 30 minutes to talk it through properly. Grab a slot here: [CALENDAR LINK] — or send me a few times that work and I'll come to you.
>
> I'll prep a short overview of how we've supported other clubs so you don't go in blind, and I can bring whichever member of the team would be most useful depending on what you want to focus on.
>
> Best wishes,
> Yasmin

## Drafting checklist (must all pass before showing the draft)
- [ ] Correct register chosen (A / B / C) — confidence stated in the draft preamble.
- [ ] Opener is `Hi [FirstName],` — not "Hello", not "Hey".
- [ ] One specific detail from their message is reflected back in the first paragraph.
- [ ] A call is offered with a clear route (Calendly link OR 2–3 slots OR ask them to send times).
- [ ] No long pitch about FCC.
- [ ] No request for a form / deck / questionnaire before the call.
- [ ] No specific commitments made (sponsorship, partnership, cohort spot).
- [ ] No client / club / partner names dropped without a `[CHECK WITH YASMIN]` flag.
- [ ] British English. Contractions throughout. No exclamation marks.
- [ ] Sign-off is `Best wishes,` then `Yasmin` on a new line.
- [ ] Length: three short paragraphs.
